Kathmandu
Kathmandu, the vibrant capital of Nepal, is a captivating blend of ancient heritage and modern energy. Nestled in the heart of the Himalayas, the city is home to centuries-old temples, bustling markets, and rich cultural traditions. From the sacred serenity of Swayambhunath Stupa to the lively alleys of Thamel, Kathmandu offers travelers a unique glimpse into Nepal’s spiritual depth and artistic legacy. Whether you're exploring UNESCO World Heritage Sites or savoring local cuisine, every corner of Kathmandu tells a story.
Bhaktapur
Bhaktapur, often called the "City of Devotees," is a living museum of Newari culture and medieval architecture. Located just east of Kathmandu, this UNESCO World Heritage Site enchants visitors with its beautifully preserved palaces, courtyards, and temples. Strolling through Bhaktapur Durbar Square feels like stepping back in time, with intricate wood carvings, traditional pottery, and vibrant festivals bringing the city’s rich history to life. It's a serene escape where heritage and craftsmanship thrive in every brick and alley.
Lalitpur
Lalitpur, traditionally known as Patan, is a city steeped in artistry and spiritual heritage. Just across the Bagmati River from Kathmandu, this ancient settlement is celebrated for its masterful craftsmanship, serene courtyards, and sacred temples. Patan Durbar Square, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, showcases a dazzling array of pagodas, shrines, and royal architecture that reflect centuries of devotion and skill. The city pulses with creativity, from intricate metal sculptures and stone carvings to vibrant festivals and living traditions.
